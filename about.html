HEY, I'M SHIVANK SHETTY.

I was born in Mumbai, India — a city where the summers are long, competition is fierce, and opportunities don’t come easy. Drawn early to the past, I chose to study archaeology at university, taking on voluntary work and freelance projects alongside my studies to build experience wherever I could.
That path eventually led me to what I thought was the “real job”: working as a researcher at an auction gallery. It paid the bills and sharpened my analytical skills, but deep down I knew it wasn’t where I was meant to stay. My family reminded me of the gifts God had given me and encouraged me to think beyond comfort and security. So I quit, bet on myself, and I chose to leave, move to the UK, and pursue archaeology properly.
I am currently completing a Master’s in Field Archaeology at the University of York, while volunteering at the Yorkshire Museum and the Oxford Museum.

But here’s the truth: 2024 was the year that knocked me flat.
It was devastating and humbling, and it stripped away any illusions I had about status or ego. It reminded me that life is ultimately about service, contribution, and integrity, not certainty. Today, I move forward with clarity: as a postgraduate archaeology student with experience in research, auctions, excavation, and coding, and a growing interest in finance — committed to building a life rooted in purpose, discipline, and long-term impact.

Three ways I can SERVE you today.

Digging the Past, Mapping the Story

I bring rigorous field training from the University of York, with strong expertise in stratigraphy, context recording, and professional excavation standards. I help transform complex site data into clear, defensible interpretations of past human activity. With GIS and spatial analysis, I map patterns of settlement, trade, and material circulation—turning physical evidence into structured historical insight suitable for both commercial and research-led archaeology.

Research That Brings the Past to Life

Research is the foundation of my work. I conduct structured investigations across historic architecture, institutional histories, and archival collections. I serve projects by handling sources with precision, and producing documentation that is methodical, and intellectually rigorous. My focus is always on connecting material evidence to wider historical and social frameworks.

Art, Data, and Market Intelligence

I combine object-based art historical research with quantitative market analysis. Working with catalogues, provenance records, and databases, I interpret both material and structured datasets. Using R for statistical analysis and visualization, I identify pricing trends, artist performance patterns, and market segmentation—delivering insights that move beyond description to strategic, evidence-based interpretation.
