About me

Archaeology

I am an archaeologist currently pursuing my Master’s in Archaeology at the University of York, with a strong focus on fieldwork, material culture, and evidence-based historical interpretation. My academic training combines excavation methodology, stratigraphic analysis, and post-excavation research with archival investigation and heritage documentation. My current focus at York is strengthening my expertise in field archaeology and professional excavation standards, with the goal of contributing to commercial and research-led archaeology in the UK. I am particularly interested in stratigraphy, context recording, and the disciplined reconstruction of past human activity through material evidence. My familiarity with GIS further expands this analytical framework. By mapping locations, historical sites, and geographic movement of trade, I examine how cultural objects circulate across regions and cities over time. Spatial analysis provides insight into networks of ownership, trade routes, and market concentration.

Research

Research is at the core of my work. I approach it as a structured process of inquiry — grounded in evidence, critical analysis, and methodological rigor. My research experience includes primary and secondary investigations of historic religious architecture, archival documentation, and material analysis. I have worked with heritage structures, studied institutional histories, and engaged with archival collections to reconstruct cultural and historical contexts. These experiences have trained me to handle sources carefully, question assumptions, and maintain precision in documentation. I am particularly interested in research that connects material evidence with broader historical and social frameworks. Whether working on site, in archives, or through textual analysis, I prioritize clarity, structure, and intellectual honesty.

Auction Analyst

My experience in the auction house environment strengthened not only my art historical research skills but also my analytical approach to data and market interpretation. Working with catalogues, provenance records, artist histories, and sales databases exposed me to structured datasets that require both critical evaluation and quantitative insight. Alongside object-based research and attribution analysis, I developed an interest in applying data tools to understand patterns within the art market. Using R for statistical analysis and data visualization, I explored trends in pricing, artist performance, sales frequency, and market segmentation. This allowed me to move beyond descriptive cataloguing toward pattern recognition and evidence-based interpretation. This integration of qualitative scholarship and quantitative analysis enables me to approach the auction environment systematically — combining material knowledge, provenance research, statistical reasoning, and spatial interpretation.
